> Collected: 2026-04-12
> Published: Unknown
> Author: Dean Arnold

## The Problem

Organizations are rapidly deploying AI agents across different platforms and vendors, creating operational challenges. According to Workday research, 82% of organizations are expanding their use of AI agents, yet lack centralized management. Core unanswered questions include: which agents are running, who owns them, are they secure, what do they cost, and are they delivering value?

## The Solution: ASOR (Generally Available)

Workday has launched its Agent System of Record (ASOR) as generally available, providing enterprises unified visibility and control over AI agents across their organizations. ASOR serves as a single system of record for all enterprise AI agents — whether built internally, by Workday, or by partners. It integrates agents into workforce strategy by measuring them as investments, governing them like employees, and improving them through training.

## Core Capabilities

1. **Unified Visibility** — Provides clear sight into where work shifts from humans to AI agents and how it connects to business outcomes.
2. **Analytics & Insights** — Delivers blended workforce analytics showing agent usage, process efficiency, ROI, and total business impact.
3. **Governance & Security** — Records and tracks all agent interactions with data, ensures appropriate access controls, and enables compliance monitoring.
4. **Open Ecosystem** — Supports 65+ global partners and 20 Workday Ventures portfolio companies using industry standards including Model Context Protocol (MCP) and OpenTelemetry.
