## Question:
What are the stand-alone AI Agent Governance or AI Workforce Management platforms that can act as a vendor-neutral 'HR system' for agents built on different platforms similar to workday asor? it must focus on "hiring" existing agents, not platforms to build agents and also not platforms to orchestrate multi agents.

## Answer:
The market for "AI Workforce Management" is moving fast, but it’s currently in its "Wild West" phase. Most tools focus on building or orchestrating, but a few specific platforms are emerging to act as that vendor-neutral HR layer—focused on the
**lifecycle, governance, and identity**
of agents rather than the code.
To fit your criteria (vendor-neutral, non-orchestration, focused on "hiring" and management), you should look at these three categories:
## 1. Agent Governance & "HR for AI" Platforms
These are the closest match to a "Workday for Agents." They treat an AI agent as a digital employee with a profile, permissions, and performance reviews.
- **[AgentOps](https://www.agentops.ai/):** While often used during development, their core value proposition is the "Observation and Governance" of agents. It acts as a compliance and HR layer where you can monitor "hired" agents, track their costs (salary), and audit their actions across different platforms.
- **[LlamaIndex (LlamaCloud)](https://www.llamaindex.ai/):** They are increasingly moving toward a "Central Control Plane." While LlamaIndex is a framework, their enterprise layer is designed to manage and observe agents built on various stacks, providing a unified dashboard for their "employment" status and efficiency.
- **[Trustible](https://www.trustible.ai/):** This is a pure-play Governance, Risk, and Compliance (GRC) platform. It doesn't build agents; it acts as the "Legal/HR department," ensuring that any agent you "hire" from an outside vendor meets your company’s internal policies and regulatory standards.
## 2. Digital Identity & Access Management (The "Badge" for Agents)
In a traditional HR system, an employee gets a login and a badge. For agents, this is being handled by "Non-Human Identity Management" (NHID) platforms.
- **[Oasis Security](https://www.oasis.security/):** They focus on the lifecycle of "Non-Human Identities." If you "hire" an agent from a third party, Oasis manages its credentials, access levels, and eventual "termination" (offboarding), exactly like an IAM-focused HR system.
- **[Skyflow](https://www.skyflow.com/):** Specifically for data privacy. It acts as a "Privacy HR" layer, ensuring that agents—regardless of who built them—only see the data they are "cleared" to see.
## 3. Agent Marketplaces (The "Recruiters")
If your focus is on
**hiring existing agents**
, these platforms function as the storefronts where you find pre-built, autonomous agents to bring into your workforce.
- **[Agent.ai](https://agent.ai/):** Driven by HubSpot’s founder, this is designed as a professional network/marketplace for agents. You browse "professionals" (agents) built by others and "hire" them to do specific tasks. It provides the profile, reviews, and integration points.
- **[OpenBPM](https://openbpm.io/):** Focuses on the "Workforce" aspect. It allows you to manage agents as part of a business process, acting as a middle-management layer that monitors output without needing to be the platform where the agent was actually coded.
## Key Distinction
The "Workday for AI" category is still forming. Most enterprises are currently using a combination of
**AgentOps**
(for performance/HR tracking) and
**Oasis**
(for identity/access).
Are you looking for this platform to primarily handle

of the agents, or are you more focused on
**measuring the ROI and productivity**
of your digital "hires"?

## Question:
what is the main goal of workday asor  legal/compliance auditing or measuring the ROI and productivity  ?   is planning a tracking expenses part of roi ?

## Answer:
The main goal of the
**Workday Agent System of Record (ASoR)**
is to
provide a
**unified management platform**
that handles both legal/compliance auditing
**and**
ROI measurement simultaneously
.
Rather than choosing between the two, ASoR treats AI agents like human employees, integrating them into the same governance and financial structures used for a human workforce.
## Dual Goals of Workday ASoR
- **Legal & Compliance Auditing:** ASoR acts as the "HR department" for agents by enforcing security permissions, tracking every interaction for accountability, and ensuring agents only access data they are cleared for. It provides a full audit trail of "who, what, and when" for every action an agent takes.
- **Measuring ROI & Productivity:** The platform uses an "Agent Analytics Hub" to track how agents impact business processes. It identifies where work shifts from humans to AI to calculate "time reclaimed" and overall process efficiency.
## Is Tracking Expenses Part of ROI?
**Yes.**
Within the Workday ecosystem, planning and tracking expenses is a critical component of the ROI equation for AI agents:
- **Cost Monitoring:** ASoR allows administrators to budget, forecast, and monitor the financial costs of each agent (e.g., subscription fees or token usage).
- **Value Comparison:** By tracking these expenses against the "value delivered" (such as hours saved or tasks completed), the system generates actionable ROI calculations for leadership.
- **Role-Specific Agents:** Workday has even released a specific **Expenses Agent** designed to automate the submission and approval of expense reports, which further helps in managing company-wide spend and improving efficiency.
