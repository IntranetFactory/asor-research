## Question 1

What are the stand-alone AI Agent Governance or AI Workforce Management platforms that can act as a vendor-neutral 'HR system' for agents built on different platforms similar to Workday ASOR? It must focus on "hiring" existing agents, not platforms to build agents and also not platforms to orchestrate multi-agents.

## Answer 1

The market for "AI Workforce Management" is in its "Wild West" phase. Most tools focus on building or orchestrating, but a few platforms are emerging to act as a vendor-neutral HR layer — focused on the lifecycle, governance, and identity of agents rather than the code.

### Category 1: Agent Governance & "HR for AI" Platforms

These are the closest match to a "Workday for Agents." They treat an AI agent as a digital employee with a profile, permissions, and performance reviews.

- **AgentOps (agentops.ai):** Core value proposition is "Observation and Governance" of agents. Acts as a compliance and HR layer where you can monitor "hired" agents, track their costs (salary), and audit their actions across different platforms. While also used during development, governance is its core.
- **LlamaIndex (LlamaCloud):** Increasingly moving toward a "Central Control Plane." The enterprise layer is designed to manage and observe agents built on various stacks, providing a unified dashboard for their "employment" status and efficiency.
- **Trustible (trustible.ai):** A pure-play Governance, Risk, and Compliance (GRC) platform. Does not build agents; acts as the "Legal/HR department," ensuring any "hired" agent from an outside vendor meets internal policies and regulatory standards.

### Category 2: Digital Identity & Access Management — "The Badge for Agents"

In traditional HR, an employee gets a login and badge. For agents, this is handled by Non-Human Identity Management (NHID) platforms.

- **Oasis Security (oasis.security):** Focuses on the lifecycle of "Non-Human Identities." Manages credentials, access levels, and "termination" (offboarding) of third-party agents, analogous to an IAM-focused HR system.
- **Skyflow (skyflow.com):** Specifically for data privacy. Acts as a "Privacy HR" layer, ensuring agents — regardless of who built them — only see data they are cleared to see.

### Category 3: Agent Marketplaces — "The Recruiters"

If the focus is on hiring existing agents, these platforms are storefronts for pre-built, autonomous agents.

- **Agent.ai:** Driven by HubSpot's founder. Designed as a professional network/marketplace for agents. Browse "professionals" (agents) built by others and "hire" them for specific tasks. Provides profiles, reviews, and integration points.
- **OpenBPM (openbpm.io):** Focuses on the "Workforce" aspect. Manages agents as part of a business process; acts as a middle-management layer that monitors output without needing to be the platform where the agent was coded.

### Market Assessment

The "Workday for AI" category is still forming. Most enterprises currently use a combination of **AgentOps** (for performance/HR tracking) and **Oasis** (for identity/access).

## Question 2

What is the main goal of Workday ASOR — legal/compliance auditing or measuring ROI and productivity? Is planning and tracking expenses part of ROI?

## Answer 2

The main goal of Workday ASoR is to provide a **unified management platform** that handles both legal/compliance auditing **and** ROI measurement simultaneously. Rather than choosing between the two, ASoR treats AI agents like human employees, integrating them into the same governance and financial structures used for a human workforce.

### Dual Goals

- **Legal & Compliance Auditing:** ASoR acts as the "HR department" for agents by enforcing security permissions, tracking every interaction for accountability, and ensuring agents only access data they are cleared for. Provides a full audit trail of "who, what, and when" for every action an agent takes.
- **Measuring ROI & Productivity:** The "Agent Analytics Hub" tracks how agents impact business processes. Identifies where work shifts from humans to AI to calculate "time reclaimed" and overall process efficiency.

### Tracking Expenses as Part of ROI

Yes. Within the Workday ecosystem, planning and tracking expenses is a critical ROI component:

- **Cost Monitoring:** ASoR allows administrators to budget, forecast, and monitor financial costs of each agent (e.g., subscription fees or token usage).
- **Value Comparison:** Tracking expenses against "value delivered" (hours saved, tasks completed) generates actionable ROI calculations for leadership.
- **Expenses Agent:** Workday has released a specific Expenses Agent to automate submission and approval of expense reports, helping manage company-wide spend and improve efficiency.
